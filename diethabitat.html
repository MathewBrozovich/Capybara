Diet

Capybaras, like most rodents, are herbivores. They eat mostly grass, with most adult capybaras eating up to eight pounds of grass per day. During dry seasons, capybaras will eat a number of substitutes, like grains, melons, and squashes.

Habitat

Capybaras typically live in large, forested areas near large bodies of water. They have a large Central and South American population, with many of the world's capybaras living near Brazil and Peru.
